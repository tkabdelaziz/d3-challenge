Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Income

The chart above is a chart that takes state data and shows the correlations between the state's poverty percentage and their obesity percentage.

It is interesting to see that there is a direct correlation between the two variables. The more the state is poverish, the more obese the population is. This can be explained by a number of reasons, but the most prominent is that healthy foods are not affordable when compared to the prices of fast food.
